FAQs

How old do you have to be to dive?

You have to be at least 10 years old to dive, be comfortable in the water, and be able to carry 30 pounds on your back.

Do I need to be certified?

There is no scuba certification needed!! Our afternoon dives are perfect for someone who wants to try scuba diving or those that have experience but no certification.

What does it mean to be Scuba Certified?

Certification simply means that you have been properly trained in the use of scuba equipment and the techniques of safe scuba diving. There are many different kinds of scuba certifications, but the certification most divers start with is the PADI Open Water Diver rating. This rating allows you to rent and buy scuba equipment, get air fills from dive centers, participate in professionally led dives, and dive in conditions that are similar to or better than the conditions you are trained in.

Can I fly right after I dive?

The short answer is no, you cannot not fly for at least 18 hours after your last dive, however we recommend 24 hours. The good news is that after you get off the plane, you can dive immediately.

Are there sharks?

We hope so! Swimming with sharks underwater is one of the most thrilling things to experience. We have never encountered an aggressive shark. Most are resting under ledges or just passing through the area.

Can I dive while pregnant?

Because physiologists know little about the effects of diving on the fetus, the recommendation is that women avoid diving while pregnant or trying to become pregnant.

Can I dive if I suffer from seasickness?

Seasickness affects everyone differently. If you’ve never been on the ocean, it’s probably a good idea to take some form of motion sickness remedy such as Dramamine or Bonine the night before and morning of your dive trip.
